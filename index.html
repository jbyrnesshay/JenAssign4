In January 2016, I went solo on a 3-day trip to Lhasa, Tibet.

The distance from my town of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania to Lhasa, Tibet is 7,556 miles.

Lhasa is actually only the 2nd most distant place I've been to.

I've also been to Kathmandu, Nepal, which is further (7629 miles), on another trip.

Lhasa is the site of a number of historic monasteries and temples.

It's one of the few large cities in Tibet but it's a small city by American standards. It's more equivalent to a large town.

From my town of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, the quickest you can do the trip to Lhasa is in about 30 hours of travel (one-way).

There are no direct flights from the United States.

Though Lhasa is modernizing,it still has historical sites and old-town areas such as Barkhor Square

The trip was challenging for a solo traveller.
